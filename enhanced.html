Georgia History Museum

Enhanced Experience Specifics

Overview

Increasing User Experience during visits through augmented reality.

Exhibits will be "alive." Each one of them will narrate their stories verbally. Users approaching a specific exhibit will be automatically contacted by the exhibit. Using audio processing, simple Natural Language Text Processing (NLTP), and machine learning, the exhibits will be able to understand visitors and answer their questions.

This method will also track the paths that visitors follow, which will allow for deep data analysis on the popularity of exhibits and the paths that feel more "natural" for visitors to go to next.

To complement this immersive environment, guards and museum staff could dress according to the historic time of the exhibition they are assigned to.

Execution

Supplying visitors with bluetooth bracelets to track their movements around the exhibits and to deliver an Augmented Reality experience.

When visitors enter the establishment, they will be supplied with the bracelets. Small bluetooth devices at every exhibition would recognize the bracelet signals and activate the Artificial Intelligence (AI) exhibit, which is equipped with a set of speakers. The exhibit then would start a conversation with the visitor(s). If interested, the visitor(s) can reply back and hear the full story from the exhibit. After hearing the story, they can also ask the exhibit more questions about its story.

The device connected to the exhibit will send the bracelet's tag and position to a central computer in the museum that will gather and process all the data for future analysis.

Data gathered can be used to rank the popularity of the exhibits based on the attention that they get, which would allow for an easier decision making process when determining the continuation of an exhibit in the museum.

Benefits & Drawbacks

Benefits

  • Increase user engagement
  • Create a natural environment for the visitor to learn from and interact with the exhibits
  • It will provide the museum with data analytics that allows for better business decisions
    • Data on visitors' paths
    • Spots to place certain exhibits at
    • Average time spent with each exhibit

Drawbacks

  • Cost. Although bluetooth devices are relatively cheap, installing them on each exhibit can be costly at a large scale
  • Lack of Space. Bluetooth devices are not very accurate, so exhibits would require an increased separating space in order to function properly
  • Accessibility. Visitors with hearing disabilities would miss on the experience
  • Participation. Because of the human nature of all the exhibits, they would not be able to handle big crowds of people. That is, they cannot respond to more than one question at the same time
