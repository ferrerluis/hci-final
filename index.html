Georgia History Museum
Web Design Specifics Enhanced Experience Specifics

Georgia History Museum

Request for Proposal

Overview

Business Objectives

  • To engage customers and increase brand loyalty.
  • To facilitate a web portal to buy tickets and souvenirs online.
  • To decrease user discomfort of not finding related information easily.

Goals

The website will provide extensive information about the museum, such as history, most popular exhibitions, museum map, and museum hours.

It will also provide functionality such as:

  • Information page (Home).
  • Registration and login service.
  • Ticket purchase page.
  • Special events.
  • Gallery page.
  • Contact us page.

Website Objectives

  • To attract online attention to the physical museum.
  • To register users, save data, and keep track of user's interactions with the features provided.
  • To complement the augmented reality system to be deployed in the museum.

Services provided by the museum

This museum will allow visitors to live the past two centuries of Georgia in a single 4-6 hour visit. The Georgia History Museum will provide fun and instructive exhibitions portraying the past of Georgia in a way never done before. Visitors can buy tickets online and save themselves the hassle of waiting in line.

Actors will be constantly walking around reenacting the past peoples of Georgia, including the native Americans that once vastly inhabited the land.

Upon entrance, visitors will be inspected for everyone's safety, and then provided with a smart bracelet that will help them throughout their learning AR voyage.

All exhibits will be free except for the temporary ones.

Projected community

The website will try to appeal any person with basic knowledge on technology and web usage.

Regarding the physical museum, main visitors can be expected to be:

  • Tourists
  • Students
  • Families

Demographics

Age Group People of any age are welcome. The interactive nature of the museum can amuse from the youngest to the oldest ones.
Gender We accept and celebrate difference. Everyone will be accepted disregarding biological gender or sexual orientation.
Nationality Tourists from all over the world are allowed and encouraged to visit the museum. Primary language will be asked when buying tickets to ensure that the visitor has an optimal experience and can learn as much as an English speaker.
(Not all languages are supported)
Education Basic knowledge on US history is advised but not required. Knowledge on the use of mobile devices is not necessary to fully enjoy the AR aspect of the visit.

Tasks

Get informed Creates online presence. Users can find all information regarding the museum, its function, the ticket buying process, and directions to the facilities on the home page.
Buy tickets Users will be able to perform purchases through the web portal to skip lines at the museum and increase UX, therefore increasing visitor turnout.
Register In order to engage the user, an account will be created before the tickets are bought, which will keep track of users using the online platform, and will help engage users by sending emails with news about the museum.
(The form will include as few questions as possible to avoid hurting UX)
Stay posted Users can visit the Special Events page to find more about the exciting exhibits that the museum has to offer at a specific point of time.
Live the experience A gallery in the website will allow users to have an basic idea of how the museum looks like and what to expect when visiting.
Ask questions There is a Contact Us page for the user to submit any question, comment, or complaint regarding the museum that could not be properly answered by the website.
